Dresden, like... it’s cool!

 

I started a new job this week in Dresden, Germany. At the Max Planck Institute - CBG to be exact. Suzie dropped in from France last weekend to help me settle in, and we had a look around town, aided by an underpowered and comical looking hired fiat panda. Despite having visited twice before, I was very pleasantly surprised to find the old town area Aldstadt full of fantastic old architecture. Much rebuilt after the war and it’s firebombing campaign, in particular the church which cost 130 million to fix up back to the original state. All very nice. Then there’s the Neustadt, where all the punks and hippies, students and other odd folk live. Think I’ll get a flat there, amidst the bars, restaurants and clubs. Quite the “happening” place apparently, lots of music and other arts.

My first week in Dresden

Friday 9 March 2007

The new job seems to be just what I wanted, in a great lab with very good science, and a nice bunch of co workers. Great! Lots of image processing customers already, good potential for the BioImageXD project, and chance to help develop and new kind of microscope... how exciting!


They put me up in the guest house on site, and I got the flat with the wide doors and “special” bathroom, despite my lack of wheelchair. Maybe I should get one to take full advantage of the quality facilities.


Hockey training starts soon, better buy some new trainers.

Already had a jam session with some blokes from the lab. I played rudimentary bass, while they did whacky improvisation stuff, which was all good fun. They kept suggesting I joined in with the improvisation. Not knowing my scales, I was restricted to modifying the rhythm a bit, having done that, I informed them that indeed I was cutting loose with the funky stuff. Then they had a long discussion about bebop and jazz improv, involving drawing diagrams on the black board. It looked like maths not music. I had a beer outside with the drummer instead. Rhythm section solidarity is key. 


Ended the week with the friday beer in the lab hour, which is a good sign for the future, means good communication between the researchers there, and the will to hand out with each other a bit. That is a good thing.


Suzie rekons the french plonk is cheaper here than it is in France, and good beer costs 60 cents a bottle. Watch out liver. No more nasty chemical Lapin Kulta, ever.


Next need to get a car... wonder if that BMW 316i compact I saw in a dodgy yard in the Neustadt for 2300e actually runs...
